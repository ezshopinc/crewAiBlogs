Understanding the Basics of Free Falling

What is Free Falling?

Free falling, in the context of skydiving, is the act of falling through the sky with the force of gravity, without any external forces or supports acting upon your body. When a skydiver jumps from an airplane, they experience a brief period of free fall during which they accelerate towards the earth at a rate of 9.8 meters per second squared. 


Defining the Term

The term free falling is derived from the concept of falling through the sky with the force of gravity, without any external forces or supports acting upon your body. When a skydiver jumps from an airplane, they experience a brief period of free fall during which they accelerate towards the earth at a rate of 9.8 meters per second squared.


Historical Context and Evolution

Skydiving as we know it today has a rich history that dates back to the late 18th century. While the modern sport of skydiving is a recreational activity that is enjoyed by thrill-seekers around the world, its origins can be traced back to the late 1700s when the first successful parachute jump was made by a French aeronaut named André-Jacques Garnerin.


The Mechanics Behind Free Falling

Physics 101: Gravity and Air Resistance

The force of gravity is the fundamental force that causes objects to fall towards the earth. When a skydiver jumps from an airplane, they accelerate towards the earth at a rate of 9.8 meters per second squared due to the force of gravity.


Newton's Laws and Their Role

Newton's first law of motion states that an object at rest will remain at rest, and an object in motion will remain in motion unless acted upon by an external force. When the skydiver is inside the airplane, they are in a state of relative rest with the airplane, and will remain at rest until they are acted upon by an external force - in this case, the force of gravity.


The Terminal Velocity Phenomenon

As the skydiver accelerates towards the earth, they will eventually reach a state of dynamic equilibrium in which the force of gravity pulling them towards the earth is balanced by the force of air resistance pushing against them. At this point, known as terminal velocity, the skydiver will no longer accelerate towards the earth, but will instead fall at a constant speed.


Free Falling in Nature

Animal Kingdom: Natural Free Fallers

Skydivers aren’t the only creatures that can experience the sensation of free falling. In fact, there are several species in the animal kingdom that are known to free fall from the sky either for the purpose of hunting, or as a means of transportation. 


Birds and Their Controlled Dives

Birds are perhaps the most well-known natural free fallers, and many species of birds are capable of diving from the sky in a manner that closely resembles the experience of free falling during a human skydive. Birds are able to free fall from the sky by tucking their wings in close to their body and diving towards the earth at high speeds. 


Insects and Their Aerial Maneuvers

Insects are another common sight in the sky, and many species of insects are capable of free falling from the sky. Insects are able to free fall from the sky by either allowing the wind to carry them, or by actively controlling their free fall.
